# AtliQ Hardwares - Sales & Finance Analytics Project

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing the sales and financial performance of AtliQ Hardwares, a global electronics manufacturing company[cite: 7]. By leveraging advanced Excel functionalities, Power Query, and Power Pivot, this project delivers actionable insights through dynamic reports to track customer performance, evaluate market targets, and analyze profit and loss (P&L) statements across various dimensions[cite: 1].

---

## 📊 Reports Included

### 1. Sales Reports
* **[Customer performance report.pdf](Customer%20performance%20report.pdf):** Tracks and evaluates net sales growth for global customers across multiple years (2019, 2020, and 2021) to identify high-performing accounts[cite: 1, 7].
* **[Market vs Target Performance report.pdf](Market%20vs%20Target%20Performance%20report.pdf):** Conducts a comprehensive comparison between actual regional sales performance and preset sales targets to pinpoint gaps and strategic opportunities[cite: 1, 8].

### 2. Finance Reports
* **[PNL by Year_2.pdf](PNL%20by%20Year.pdf):** Provides a high-level view of Net Sales, Cost of Goods Sold (COGS), Gross Margin (GM), and Gross Margin % across fiscal periods[cite: 1, 11].
* **[PNL by Quarters.pdf](PNL%20by%20Quarters.pdf):** Breaks down the financial metrics into specific fiscal months and quarters to track seasonal trends and monthly growth[cite: 10].
* **[PNL by Markets_2.pdf](PNL%20by%20Markets.pdf):** Evaluates regional financial performance, showcasing Net Sales, COGS, and Gross Margin % for over 20 countries[cite: 1, 9].

## 📈 Key Insights & Project Findings

Based on the completed dashboards, here are some of the critical data insights extracted from the reports:

* **Massive Top-line Growth:** AtliQ Hardwares' overall Net Sales grew exponentially from **$87.5M** in 2019 to **$598.9M** in 2021—a massive **204.48%** increase between 2020 and 2021 alone[cite: 11].
* **Gross Margin Compression:** While sales scaled up significantly, the Gross Margin (GM %) steadily compressed from **41.43%** (2019) to **37.28%** (2020) and down to **36.43%** (2021)[cite: 11]. This indicates rising production or operational costs (COGS grew by 208.59% in 2021)[cite: 11].
* **Top Markets:** **India** is the largest market by revenue, bringing in **$161.3M** in net sales for 2021[cite: 8], followed by the **USA** at **$87.8M**[cite: 8]. 
* **Target Gaps:** Globally, the company missed its total 2021 target by **-$54.9M** (-8.40%)[cite: 8]. Specifically, Poland (-15.35%) and Canada (-12.63%) showed the highest percentage deficit against targets[cite: 8].
* **Most Profitable Regions:** In terms of Gross Margin %, **New Zealand (44.98%)**, **Japan (44.68%)**, and the **Netherlands (43.59%)** lead the chart, making them highly lucrative zones despite lower total volume[cite: 9].
* **Top Customers:** **Amazon** ($82.1M in 2021), **AtliQ Exclusive** ($61.1M), and **AtliQ e Store** ($53.0M) are the primary drivers of customer net sales[cite: 7].
